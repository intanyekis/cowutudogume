Alice Merton Biography and Wiki

Born on September 13th, 1993, in Frankfurt, Germany, Alice Merton is a German-Canadian- British singer and Songwriter. Alice rose to fame for her debut single, ‘No Roots’. At first, Merton was a singer and songwriter of the Fahrenhaidt band in 2015.

Alice Merton Education

Merton attended a German-language high school and later after graduating she moved to England. Additionally, Alice is an alma mater of the Popakademie Baden-Württemberg in Mannheim. Alice Merton Singer-Songwriter

Merton first rose to fame in 2015 as a songwriter and singer on  Fahrenhaidt’s album The Book of Nature. Alice won her first award for the promotion of talented newcomers in the category Acoustic Pop. Merton worked with Paul Grauwinkel to form the record label Paper Plane Records International.

On 18 October 2019, Merton released her album Mint again with four additional songs (Back To Berlin, PCH, Easy and Keeps Me Awake). Later, at the beginning of 2020, she toured across Germany, Austria, Czech Republic, and Denmark singing these new songs, as well as the ones from her previous album.

Alice Merton No Roots

The duo released the song ‘No Roots’ at the end of 2016. ‘No Roots’ song lyrics were inspired by her feeling of ‘There’s no one place where my home is’. The song reached number one on both Hype Machine and Spotify Global Viral 50 Charts.

Merton first released her first EP, No Roots, on February 3rd, 2017. Alice performed at the Life Ball in Vienna on June 10th, 2017. Later on, on September 7th, 2018, Alice released ‘Why So Serious’ the lead single for her debut album ‘Mint’.
