Blac Chyna Sells Her Personal Belongings To Make Ends Meet Amid Custody War With Tyga

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, May 25, 2024

Highlights

  • Blac Chyna, once a top creator on OnlyFans, is now selling her personal belongings to make extra income due to financial problems.
  • She claims to have made $178,000 from selling her things but warns that it's only temporary as she'll eventually run out of belongings to sell.
  • Chyna blames her poor financial situation on rising legal bills from an ongoing custody dispute with Tyga and is seeking $125,000 from him to cover her legal expenses.

Blac Chyna was once one of OnlyFans’ top creators, but it hasn’t stopped the Internet personality from running into financial problems, which has recently prompted Chyna to begin selling her personal belongings.

Chyna is currently in a custody and child support dispute with rapper Tyga over their 8-year old son King Cairo. As per court documents obtained by TMZ, the reality star claims she’s begun selling old clothes and accessories via an online consignment boutique in order to make extra income.

Although Chyna claims to have made $178,000 in the last year from selling her things, she warned the court that the revenue is only temporary, since she’ll eventually run out of belongings to sell. Chyna blames her poor financial situation on the rising legal bills she’s incurring from the ongoing custody dispute.

The content creator is only entitled to 24-hours of visitation time with her son per week, which she says Tyga has been meddling with, thus preventing her from seeing her son for the full time she’s supposed to be allotted. She also claims her ex is withholding important information about their son, including the location of where he lives and goes to school as well as info about his health, like prescriptions.

Chyna is hoping to win $125,000 from Tyga to go towards her mounting legal bills. She also wants to establish a new custody schedule that Tyga will be unable to interrupt.

Chyna’s financial problems are shocking, given that it was once reported she was making $20 million per month on OnlyFans. Reports from 2021 claim she made $240 million.

However, Chyna later revealed she was stepping away from the platform, as she was no longer comfortable with the type of content – meaning the entire source of revenue has ceased for her.

“That’s another reason why I wanted to step out of some of the things that I was doing. At a certain age they [kids] see and gravitate to everything,” Chyna, whose real name is Angela White, explained. “Like for example, I don’t do OnlyFans anymore. I don’t do it anymore at all.”

Chyna has accused Tyga as well as her younger daughter’s father Rob Kardashian of failing to pay child support. But both celebs clapped back at her claims, insisting they care for their kids the majority of the time, including financially.

Chyna’s custody war with Tyga remains ongoing, as do her apparent financial problems.
