Who Is The Richest Of The 'Sons Of Anarchy' Cast?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, July 24, 2024

More than eight years since it concluded its story, the FX drama Sons of Anarchy continues to be praised by many, thanks to the show’s availability on Hulu.Sons of Anarchy centers on a motorcycle club that patrols to keep drug dealers out of Charming, California. The group famously included Charlie Hunnam’s Jax Teller, Mark Boone Junior’s Robert ‘Bobby Elvis’ Munson, and Ron Perlman’s Clarence ‘Clay’ Morrow.Throughout its run, the show drew praise from critics, ultimately earning five Emmy nods.RELATED: What Happened To Ryan Hurst’s Career After Playing Opie On ‘Sons Of Anarchy' For the cast, the show gave them a great deal of exposure, which is exactly what they needed to land other roles. In fact, over the years, Hunnam, Boone, Perlman, and the rest of the cast have been plenty busy, appearing on multiple TV shows and in other projects.Of course, not all of them hit the big time; fans have wondered what happened to Ryan Hurst's career post-Sons because he seemed to disappear from view.But years later, one can’t help but wonder which Sons of Anarchy star has earned the most today.

Drea De Matteo Is Worth $15 To $18 Million Today

De Matteo, who famously played Wendy Case, had been an established actress long before she booked a role on the show. In fact, in the past, she even starred in the Friends spinoff Joey as Joey’s (Matt LeBlanc) sister Gina.

Fans may also remember de Matteo’s portrayal of Adriana La Cerva in the hit HBO series The Sopranos. The actress also won an Emmy for her performance on the show.

Following Sons of Anarchy, de Matteo appeared briefly in the Marvel series Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. as Karla Faye Gideon. Soon after, she starred in Shades of Blue alongside Jennifer Lopez. The actress then went on to star in A Million Little Things and more recently, Paradise City.

Charlie Hunnam And Ron Perlman Are Each Worth An Impressive $20 Million

Both Hunnam and Perlman had prominent roles in the series throughout its run (although Perlman’s character was killed off much earlier than Hunnam’s). And when it ended, both went on to do several major projects.

For instance, Hunnam decided to do more films, starring in the Lost City of Z, Guy Ritchie’s King Arthur: Legend of the Sword, A Million Little Pieces, Children of Men, The Ledge, and Papillon among others. Hunnam also joined the Netflix action film Triple Frontier, which stars Ben Affleck, Oscar Isaac, and Pedro Pascal.

From the beginning, Hunnam knew the film delves deeper into PTSD and that’s why he took on the role.

“It was my intuition before I even read the script. That’s what interested me about this world,” the actor told amNY.

“What do you do when you have dedicated your life to a certain skill set and through that belonging to a really deep sense of community … What do you do when all of that basically gets taken away from you?”

As for Perlman, the actor starred in various films after exiting Sons of Anarchy. These include Chuck, Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them, Poker Night, Pottersville, and Asher among others.

The actor also did some voice acting for the animated film The Book of Life and the animated shows Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, Adventure Time, Transformers: Power of the Primes, and Netflix’s Trollhunters: Tales of Arcadia.

Aside from these, Perlman received critical acclaim for his performance as Pernell Harris in the Amazon crime drama Hand of God. It was a role that the actor pursued as soon as he learned that Clay was going to get killed off.

“When I found out that Sons was coming to an end for me, I began looking and reading stuff,” Perlman told Esquire. “Hand of God was the third script I read. I'd never seen anything that encompassed a discussion as vast as this one.”

She May Not Have Been A Member Of The Biker Club, But Katey Sagal Takes The Top Spot

Once she was done working on the show, Segal simply kept going. For starters, she immediately got to work on other TV shows, including The Big Bang Theory, This is Us, Superior Donuts, Brooklyn Nine-Nine, Shameless, and even the Sons of Anarchy spinoff Mayans M.C. where she reprised her role briefly.

At the same time, Sagal briefly starred in the hit Netflix series Dead to Me. For series creator Liz Feldman, the actress’ casting couldn’t have been more perfect.

“We were looking for somebody that you could really believe would be Judy’s mom, so somebody who looks somewhat like her,” Feldman told TVLine. “But also somebody that could really balance out the comedy and her grit that we were looking for in this woman.”

Aside from these TV projects, Sagal did some film work, appearing in Pitch Perfect 2 and Bleed for This, among others. Today, the actress is said to be worth between $30 and $35 million. And yes, she is currently the richest among the Sons of Anarchy stars.
