The Most Expensive Porsches Ever Sold, Ranked

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, June 4, 2024

Quick Links

  • Porsche 911 GT1 Road Car – $2.2 Million
  • Porsche 911 Carrera RSR 3.0 IROC – $2.3 Million
  • Porsche Rinspeed Bedouin – $2.5 Million
  • Porsche 718 RSK – $2.86 Million
  • Porsche 917 Can-Am Spyder – $3 Million
  • Porsche 917 – $3.9 Million
  • Porsche Mission E – $4 Million
  • Porsche 550 Spyder – $6 Million

In the world of luxury cars, Porsche has long been synonymous with elegance, performance, and exclusivity. With a rich heritage spanning over seven decades, the German automaker has consistently pushed the boundaries of automotive engineering and design. Among the countless iconic models that have rolled off their production lines, a select few have achieved a level of desirability and rarity that sets them apart from the rest. Let’s explore the 8 most expensive Porsche cars ever sold.

Porsche 911 GT1 Road Car – $2.2 Million

Throughout its history, Porsche has consistently prioritized the development of their racing cars, crafting them before the creation of their road counterparts. The same principle held true for this particular model. Originally designed to compete in the GT1 race class, the 911 GT1 initially faced challenges and did not achieve immediate success.

Finally, in 1998, they hit the mark, and the 911 GT1 emerged as a true champion on the racetrack, showcasing its remarkable capabilities. The manufacturer produced only a restricted number of these exceptional vehicles. Remarkably, one of these exclusive Porsches was sold for an astonishing $2.2 million, solidifying its status as one of the most expensive models ever sold.

Porsche 911 Carrera RSR 3.0 IROC – $2.3 Million

After racing regulations deemed the existing Porsche 917 unsuitable, Porsche was compelled to devise a fresh design for its victorious model. Consequently, in 1974, Porsche introduced the Porsche 911 Carrera RSR 3.0 IROC. This new iteration boasted several notable distinctions from its predecessor.

Underneath its aerodynamic exterior, the car housed a robust 3.0-liter 2-cylinder SOHC engine, capable of generating 330 horsepower. This powerplant ensured exhilarating performance on the road and the racetrack. One of these splendid cars commanded a remarkable price of $2.3 million, securing its place as the seventh most expensive Porsche ever sold.

Porsche Rinspeed Bedouin – $2.5 Million

Among Porsche's most distinctive designs, the Porsche Rinspeed Bedouin stands out with its exceptional features, including a truly unique way to instantly expand cabin space. With a simple touch of a button, this car can seamlessly transform from a four-seater wagon to a two-seater pick-up-and-go vehicle in a mere two seconds.

Not only does the Rinspeed Bedouin offer customizable seating options, but it also excels in fuel efficiency, showcasing Porsche's commitment to eco-friendliness. Originally unveiled at the Geneva Motor Show in 2003, the Rinspeed Bedouin has captivated attention over the years. Now, after 20 years since its introduction, one of these extraordinary Porsche vehicles was sold for an impressive $2.5 million, solidifying its status as a prized collectible.

Porsche 718 RSK – $2.86 Million

A mere 37 units of the Porsche 718 RSK were ever crafted, making it an incredibly rare and coveted gem. This model is widely regarded as the closest iteration to Porsche's initial race car, the Porsche 550. The development of the 718 RSK aimed to preserve the essence and excellence of the legendary 550, further refining its brilliance and integrity.

The original RSK made its debut in 1959, widely acknowledged as the pinnacle year for this model. Notably, one of these remarkable vehicles was previously owned by the esteemed actor, Jerry Seinfeld. However, Seinfeld auctioned off the Porsche to a fortunate new owner, who reportedly paid a staggering $2.86 million to acquire this unforgettable piece of automotive history.

Porsche 917 Can-Am Spyder – $3 Million

Among the remarkable vehicles in Jerry Seinfeld's collection, the Porsche 917 Can-Am Spyder holds a prominent position. Unlike many other Porsches primarily built for endurance races, this particular model was specifically crafted to compete in the North American Can-Am Challenge race series. The demanding nature of this series necessitated specific modifications to the car's construction.

To meet the requirement for a more potent engine, Porsche equipped the 917 Can-Am Spyder with a remarkable 5.4-liter V12 turbocharged engine, delivering an astounding 1,580 horsepower to the driver. Notably, Seinfeld chose to part ways with this extraordinary Porsche at a Gooding & Co. auction, where it was sold for a staggering $3 million to an enthusiastic bidder and automotive enthusiast.

Porsche 917 – $3.9 Million

This particular Porsche carries a captivating tale and has undergone several transformations throughout its history. Originally, it was a 1970 model Gulf-JWA Le Mans 917K Coupe, specifically identified as number 026. During the 1970 Le Mans race, the car was driven by Mike Hailwood and David Hobbs. However, an intense downpour triggered a severe crash, necessitating extensive repairs.

Equipped with a formidable 5.4-liter flat-12 cylinder engine, this Porsche delivers a commanding 630 horsepower. Notably, it was sold at a Monterey auction in 2010, commanding an impressive price of $3.9 million dollars. The substantial sum reflects the rich history and unique characteristics of this iconic Porsche.

Porsche Mission E – $4 Million

Porsche Mission E is a truly remarkable and visually stunning model. This vehicle showcases one of the sleekest designs ever produced by Porsche. What sets it apart even further is the fact that it represents Porsche's inaugural venture into the realm of all-electric sports cars, denoted by the letter "E" in its name.

The Porsche Mission E boasts a cutting-edge 600 horsepower 800-volt lithium battery, demonstrating the brand's commitment to high performance and innovation. With a single battery charge, this car can cover over 500 kilometers. Utilizing the Porsche Turbo Charging system, the Mission E can be fully charged in just 15 minutes, an achievement that truly stands out.

Porsche 550 Spyder – $6 Million

Here is the ultimate pinnacle of Porsche's most expensive cars ever sold, and it's none other than the renowned Porsche 550 Spyder. This extraordinary vehicle fetched an astounding price of $6 million at the Bonhams Goodwood Revival Sale, solidifying its legendary status.

It was crafted with a 1.5-liter 2-cylinder DOHC engine, which, during its time, was considered an impressive powerhouse. This engine undoubtedly contributed to the car's reputation as a thrilling performer. Without a doubt, this iconic model stands as the most expensive Porsche ever sold, forever etching its name in the annals of automotive history.
